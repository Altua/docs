# Third-party and subcontractor policy

## Version history

| Version | Effective date | Owner | Summary |
|---------|----------------|-------|---------|
| 1.0 | April 10, 2026 | Engineering | Initial documented third-party and subcontractor policy. |

## Purpose

This policy defines how Grunt AS manages access to data by third parties, subcontractors, and external service providers.

## Scope

This policy applies to all non-employees who may have access to Grunt AS systems, data, or customer information, including subcontractors, data center providers, cloud service providers, and AI service providers.

## Data access restrictions

- No third parties, subcontractors, or external service providers are permitted to access, retain, or use customer data to train models or improve their own services
- Third-party access to Grunt AS systems or data is limited to what is strictly necessary for the contracted service
- Third parties must not store or process customer data beyond the scope of their engagement

## Due diligence

Before engaging a third party that will have access to company or customer data:

- The third party's security practices are evaluated
- Contractual provisions are established covering confidentiality, data use limitations, breach notification, and data destruction upon termination
- The engagement is approved by management

## Monitoring and oversight

- Third-party access is reviewed periodically to confirm it remains necessary and appropriately scoped
- Any material changes to a third party's services, ownership, or security posture are evaluated for impact

## AI and model training

Customer data must not be used by any third party, including AI service providers, for model training, service improvement, or any purpose beyond the contracted service.

## Roles and responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| Management | Approve third-party engagements, oversee compliance |
| Engineering | Implement and enforce technical access controls |
| CTO | Review third-party security assessments |

## Review

This policy is reviewed annually and when new third-party engagements are established.
