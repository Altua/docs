---
title: "Enable SSO for an organization"
description: "Steps to enable Microsoft Entra single sign-on for a customer organization"
---

This guide covers how to enable Microsoft Entra ID (formerly Azure AD) single sign-on for a customer organization.

Once enabled, users whose email domain matches the organization's configured domain will be redirected to Microsoft Entra SSO when signing in, instead of using the standard email validation flow.

## Prerequisites

- Admin access to the backoffice solution
- The customer's email domain (e.g. `example.com`)

## Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set the organization domain">
    In the backoffice solution, open the customer's organization and fill in the **Domain** field with the customer's email domain (e.g. `example.com`).

    This is the domain that will be matched against the user's email address during sign-in.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable the MicrosoftSignIn feature flag">
    In the backoffice solution, navigate to the organization's feature flags. Add and enable the **MicrosoftSignIn** feature flag.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How it works

After both steps are completed:

1. A user opens Grunt and enters their email address.
2. Grunt checks the domain of the email address against organizations with SSO enabled.
3. If the domain matches, the user is redirected to Microsoft Entra SSO instead of the standard email validation.
4. After successful authentication with Entra, the user is signed in to Grunt.

## Troubleshooting

- **SSO not triggering** — Verify that the domain field is filled in correctly and matches the user's email domain exactly. Confirm the MicrosoftSignIn feature flag is enabled.
- **User gets standard sign-in instead of SSO** — The email domain may not match. Double-check the domain configured for the organization.
- **Authentication fails at Microsoft** — This is on the customer's Entra ID side. Ask the customer's IT department to verify their Entra configuration.
