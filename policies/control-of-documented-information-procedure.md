## Purpose

This procedure defines how Grunt AS controls documented information required to support the information security management system (ISMS).

It ensures that ISMS documents and records are:

- Approved for adequacy and accuracy before use
- Reviewed and updated when needed
- Version controlled
- Available where needed
- Protected from unauthorized access, loss, damage, misuse, or alteration
- Distributed, accessed, retrieved, and used in a controlled way
- Retained or archived when required for legal, audit, compliance, or knowledge preservation purposes
- Securely disposed of when retention is no longer required

## Scope

This procedure applies to documented information related to the Grunt AS ISMS, including:

- Policies
- Procedures
- Standards
- Guidelines
- ISMS management documents
- Information security policies
- Evidence
- Records
- Reports
- Artifacts used to support security, privacy, compliance, audit, or certification activities

The ISMS scope is defined in the ISMS scope policy.

## Document repositories and source of record

Grunt AS maintains ISMS documents in controlled electronic repositories.

The source of record for policy and procedure documents is the company version-controlled documentation repository.

Vanta may be used to track ISMS control evidence, compliance status, document references, and audit evidence.

SharePoint or other approved systems may be used to store supporting records and archived evidence when appropriate.

Documents must be stored in locations that provide access control, retrievability, and protection against unauthorized alteration or deletion.

## Master list of documents

Grunt AS maintains a master list of ISMS documents.

The master list identifies current ISMS management documents, information security policies, and related controlled documents.

The ISMS owner is responsible for keeping the master list accurate and aligned with the latest approved document versions.

The master list should include, where applicable:

- Document title
- Document owner
- Current version
- Effective date
- Review cadence
- Approval status
- Repository or location
- Document origin, such as internal or external
- Related policies or procedures

## External documents

External documents are documents created or maintained outside Grunt AS that are needed to plan, operate, maintain, or demonstrate the ISMS.

External documents may include:

- Laws, regulations, and regulatory guidance
- ISO standards and certification requirements
- Customer security requirements
- Supplier security documentation
- Cloud provider shared responsibility documentation
- Vendor contracts, data processing agreements, and security addenda
- Penetration test reports from third parties
- Independent audit reports and certifications
- Insurance documents
- Product or platform documentation used to configure or operate in-scope systems

External documents that are relevant to the ISMS must have an assigned internal owner.

The owner is responsible for:

- Identifying whether the external document is relevant to the ISMS
- Recording the document in the master list or another approved tracking location when it is material to the ISMS
- Recording the source, owner, version or publication date, and retrieval location where available
- Confirming that the document is current before it is used for ISMS decisions or evidence
- Reviewing the document at least annually, or when the external source publishes a material update
- Assessing whether changes affect Grunt AS policies, controls, risks, suppliers, or compliance obligations
- Retaining or archiving evidence of the document when required for audit, legal, contractual, or compliance purposes

When possible, Grunt AS should reference the authoritative source rather than maintaining uncontrolled local copies.

If a local copy is needed, the copy must include enough context to identify the source, retrieval date, and version or publication date.

External documents must be protected according to their sensitivity and contractual requirements.

Outdated external documents must not be used as current guidance. They may be retained as historical evidence if needed for audit, legal, compliance, or knowledge preservation purposes.

## Document format

ISMS documents are maintained electronically.

If hard copies are required, personnel should print directly from the approved source of record to reduce the risk of using outdated information.

Printed documents and locally saved copies are considered uncontrolled unless explicitly approved and controlled by the ISMS owner.

## Document structure

ISMS documents should include the following information where applicable:

- Document name
- Version history
- Effective date
- Owner
- Purpose
- Scope
- Requirements, procedure, or control description
- Roles and responsibilities
- Related policies or resources
- Review requirements

The level of detail may vary based on the type and purpose of the document.

## Naming conventions

ISMS management documents should use a clear and descriptive name.

Legacy ISMS management documents may use the following format:

```text
##-ISMS Document Name
```

In that format:

- `##` is the document number, such as `01` or `05`
- `ISMS` means information security management system
- `Document Name` is a meaningful description of the document

Information security policies should use a concise descriptive name, such as `Risk management policy` or `Data management policy`.

Repository file names should use lowercase words separated by hyphens.

## Ownership, approval, and issuance

Each ISMS document must have an owner.

The owner is responsible for maintaining the document, coordinating review, and proposing updates when needed.

Management or the ISMS owner approves ISMS policy and procedure changes before they take effect.

The Information Security Officer may prepare, update, and maintain ISMS documents on behalf of management or the ISMS governance function.

Approved documents are issued by making the approved version available in the source of record and, where applicable, by updating Vanta or the master list of documents.

## Review and version control

ISMS documents must be reviewed at least annually and when major changes occur.

Major changes may include:

- Changes to ISMS scope
- Changes to legal, regulatory, contractual, customer, or certification requirements
- Significant organizational or ownership changes
- Significant changes to systems, suppliers, products, services, or processes
- Significant incidents, audit findings, or risk assessment findings

Document updates must follow the change management process for non-technical ISMS changes.

When a document is changed, the owner must:

- Update the version history
- Update the effective date when appropriate
- Update related documents, references, or the master list when needed
- Obtain required approval before the change takes effect
- Communicate material changes to relevant personnel

Version numbers should be updated when significant changes are made.

Minor editorial corrections may be made without changing the major version when they do not change meaning, requirements, ownership, scope, or responsibilities.

## Distribution, access, retrieval, and use

ISMS documents must be available to personnel who need them for their work.

Distribution may occur through:

- The documentation repository
- Vanta
- SharePoint
- Internal communication channels
- Direct communication to affected personnel

Access to ISMS documents and records must be controlled according to business need, data classification, and the sensitivity of the information.

Personnel must use the current approved version of a document when performing ISMS-related activities.

## Protection of documented information

ISMS documents and records must be protected from:

- Unauthorized access
- Unauthorized alteration
- Accidental loss or deletion
- Damage
- Misuse
- Confusion with outdated versions

Protection methods may include:

- Repository access controls
- Change history
- Pull request review or approval workflows
- Vanta evidence controls
- SharePoint permissions
- Backup or retention controls
- Audit trails where available

## Archival, retention, and disposal of documents

Approved documents that are no longer current should be archived when needed to preserve an audit trail or institutional knowledge.

Archived security policies and documents are retained according to the Data management policy.

When an ISMS document is no longer required and its retention period has expired, it must be securely deleted or disposed of according to company policy.

Archived copies should be clearly distinguishable from current approved versions.

## Records

Records, reports, and artifacts may be added to the ISMS documentation repository or another approved system when needed.

Examples include:

- Risk assessment reports
- Internal audit reports
- Audit evidence
- Control evidence
- Asset inventories
- Vulnerability reports
- Screenshots supporting evidence
- Security review artifacts
- Incident or root cause analysis records

### Record naming

Records do not require a strict naming convention.

Records should use descriptive names where possible.

Examples include:

- `laptop inventory`
- `application log`
- `screenshot of login screen`
- `risk assessment report`
- `internal audit report`

### Record storage and retrieval

Records may be stored and retrieved through:

- The tool or system that creates them
- Text files
- Image files
- Web browser links to source pages
- Vanta
- SharePoint
- The documentation repository when appropriate

Records should be retrievable by authorized personnel when needed for operations, management review, audit, compliance, or certification activities.

### Record archival, retention, and disposal

Records exceeding their useful life may be stored in an approved archive to preserve an audit trail.

Archived records must be maintained for at least one year unless a longer period is required by the Data management policy, legal obligation, contractual requirement, audit need, or business requirement.

When records are no longer required and their retention period has expired, they must be securely deleted or disposed of according to company policy.

## Classification

ISMS documents and records must be classified and handled according to the Data management policy.

Documents or records containing customer data, personal data, security findings, credentials, secrets, incident details, audit findings, or sensitive business information must receive appropriate protection based on their classification.

## Roles and responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| Management | Approve ISMS policy and procedure changes where management approval is required. |
| ISMS owner | Maintain the master list of documents, coordinate document reviews, and ensure controlled documents remain current. |
| Information Security Officer | Prepare, update, maintain, and communicate ISMS documents on behalf of management or the ISMS governance function. |
| Document owner | Maintain assigned documents, update version history, coordinate review, and communicate material changes. |
| Record owner | Ensure records are stored, retrievable, protected, and retained according to applicable requirements. |
| Employees and contractors | Use current approved documents and follow document handling, classification, and retention requirements. |

## Appendix A - Controlled document checklist

Use this checklist when creating or updating ISMS documents.

| Item | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| Document name | Use a clear name that describes the document. |
| Owner | Assign a document owner. |
| Version history | Record the version, effective date, owner, and change summary. |
| Purpose | Explain why the document exists. |
| Scope | Define what the document applies to. |
| Requirements or procedure | State the requirements, process, or controls clearly. |
| Responsibilities | Identify responsible roles where applicable. |
| Related policies | List related documents where useful. |
| Approval | Obtain required approval before the document takes effect. |
| Distribution | Make the current approved version available to relevant personnel. |
| Retention | Confirm retention requirements for documents and related records. |

## Appendix B - ISO 27701 PIMS addendum

This addendum applies if Grunt AS implements ISO/IEC 27701 or extends the ISMS into a privacy information management system (PIMS).

When the addendum applies:

- References to `ISMS` should be read as references to the information security and privacy management system
- References to ISO/IEC 27001 should be read as references to ISO/IEC 27001 and ISO/IEC 27701
- References to the information security management system should be read as references to the information security and privacy management system
