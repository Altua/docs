## Purpose

This policy defines the scope of the Grunt AS information security management system (ISMS).

Grunt AS has established an ISMS aligned with ISO/IEC 27001 to protect company assets, customer information, supporting services, and business operations.

## ISMS scope statement

The Grunt AS ISMS covers the assets, people, technologies, locations, suppliers, and processes used to develop, sell, deliver, support, and operate Grunt AS software products and supporting services.

The ISMS includes controls and processes needed to protect the confidentiality, integrity, availability, and privacy of customer data, company information, and supporting systems.

The scope is defined with consideration of:

- The external and internal context of Grunt AS
- Requirements from interested parties, including customers, personnel, auditors, regulators, and business partners
- Interfaces and dependencies with third parties
- In-scope assets, systems, locations, departments, and personnel
- Scope exclusions and shared responsibility boundaries

## In-scope activities

The ISMS applies to activities related to:

- Product development and software delivery
- Product operations and support
- Customer onboarding and customer success
- Sales and customer relationship management
- Security, compliance, and risk management
- Finance and business administration where these activities affect information security
- Supplier management for services that support in-scope systems or data

## In-scope assets

The ISMS covers the following asset categories:

- Information assets
- Application assets
- Database assets
- Cloud infrastructure and cloud file hosting
- Personnel assets
- Documentation, procedures, and operational knowledge
- Third-party services that support in-scope systems, data, or business processes

Detailed in-scope assets are listed in Appendix D.

The following departments are in scope:

- Technology
- Sales

- Customer Success (CS)
- Marketing
- Finance

All personnel belonging to these departments are in scope when they access, manage, develop, support, sell, or operate Grunt AS systems, products, customer data, company data, or supporting services.

## Scope exclusions and boundaries

Grunt AS uses Microsoft Azure and other third-party providers for cloud computing, hosting, collaboration, identity, communication, source code management, customer relationship management, and support workflows.

These providers are responsible for the parts of their services that remain under their control, including:

- Physical security and environmental controls for provider-operated facilities
- Physical network, storage, and compute infrastructure operated by the provider
- Secure disposal of provider-owned equipment
- Provider-managed service availability and platform security controls, according to the applicable contract and shared responsibility model

Grunt AS remains responsible for:

- Access controls and identity management for Grunt AS users
- Secure configuration of in-scope cloud services and SaaS platforms
- Protection of customer data and company data stored or processed in third-party services
- Vendor risk management and contractual controls
- Secure software development and change management
- Incident response, risk management, and ISMS governance

Information, application, database, facility, personnel, and supplier assets that are not related to the in-scope activities, systems, products, services, or departments are outside the ISMS scope.

## ISO 27001 coverage

This scope supports ISO/IEC 27001 clauses and controls related to:

- Clause 4.1 - Understanding the organization and its context
- Clause 4.2 - Understanding the needs and expectations of interested parties
- Clause 4.3 - Determining the scope of the ISMS
- Clause 7.5.1 - Documented information
- Annex A 7.1 - Physical security perimeters

## Scope review and maintenance

The ISMS owner reviews the ISMS scope at least annually and when material changes occur.

Material changes may include:

- New products, services, systems, or infrastructure
- Significant supplier or third-party changes
- New locations or major changes to work arrangements
- Organizational changes affecting in-scope departments or responsibilities
- Changes to legal, regulatory, contractual, customer, or certification requirements
- Significant security incidents or risk assessment findings

Changes to the ISMS scope must be reviewed, approved, and documented according to the change management procedure.

## Appendix E - External and internal issues

ISO/IEC 27001 clause 4.1 requires Grunt AS to determine external and internal issues relevant to its purpose and ability to achieve intended ISMS outcomes.

Grunt AS reviews these issues as part of management review and risk management.

### External issues

External issues are outside Grunt AS control.

| Issue | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Regulatory requirements | Compliance with GDPR and other data protection laws relevant to Grunt AS operations. |
| Technological changes | Rapid evolution of cloud technologies and the need to adapt security controls. |
| Cybersecurity threat landscape | Increasing frequency and sophistication of cyber attacks targeting SaaS providers. |
| Supplier and partner dependencies | Reliance on third-party cloud service providers and their security posture. |

### Internal issues

Internal issues are subject to Grunt AS control.

| Issue | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Company culture | Emphasis on agility and rapid product development, which may introduce security risk if not managed. |
| Knowledge and skills | Varying levels of security awareness and technical expertise among staff. |
| Resource constraints | Limited personnel and budget for dedicated security roles. |
| Business processes | Integration of security into software development lifecycle and change management processes. |
